Asteroid Collision Avoidance Strategies

As you have seen in the simulation that an asteroid can caused devastating impact on populations of the earth. This page contains asteroid collision avoidance techniques studied or used by NASA.

Kinetic Impactor

Description: A spacecraft deliberately crashes into the asteroid at high speed, nudging its orbit slightly.
Impact: Even a small velocity change (fractions of a cm/s) can make the asteroid miss Earth if applied years in advance.
Key Point: Tested by NASA's DART mission in 2022, showing real feasibility.

Nuclear Detonation

Description: A nuclear device detonated near the asteroid vaporizes part of its surface, producing thrust that pushes it off course.
Impact: Offers a much stronger deflection than a kinetic impactor, making it effective even with shorter warning times.
Key Point: Politically controversial, with risks of asteroid fragmentation.

Back Next

Gravity Tractor

Description: A spacecraft hovers close to the asteroid, using its own gravitational pull to slowly tug the asteroid onto a new trajectory.
Impact: Requires many years or decades to be effective, but offers precise, controlled deflection without breaking up the asteroid.
Key Point: Gentle but time-sensitive method.
